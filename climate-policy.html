The problem

Global Warming

In recent years we have been going through climate change and it looks like it’s just getting worse. Effects of climate change in NZ include extreme rain, droughts, wildfire and sea level rise and if not taken seriously may submerge NZ underwater in coming years.

The biggest contributors to climate change in NZ are transportation and electricity production. Transportation roughly emits 27% of greenhouse gas emissions and electricity production emits 25% greenhouse gas emissions. These numbers are really high and it’s our priority to lower these.

Our Plan

Eliminating greenhouse gas emissions

Here is our plan that will help with greenhouse gas emissions and effects of climate change in NZ

  • Improve public transport
  • Give a $10,000 bonus to whoever purchases an electric vehicle
  • Reduce the electricity production of non-renewable sources and increase renewable energy

    • Improve public transport

    At React NZ we think that public transport is really underused compared to the amount of cars on the road. We will be making buses more accessible by making stops at convenient places and making sure buses are less cancelled. We will also be making separate bus lanes for easy travel between places without traffic. Public transport prices will also be cut by 25%.

    Give a $10,000 bonus to whoever purchases an electric vehicle

    This will convince people to purchase an electric vehicle than a gas powered one. We will also be supporting EV manufacturers to sell these vehicles which will lower the greenhouse gas emissions by a huge amount.

    Reduce the electricity production of non-renewable sources and increase renewable energy

    Increasing the use of renewable energy will make sure that we have less global warming, stable energy prices and improved public health. As most renewable energy sources can operate at very low costs means that we will have stable energy prices. Along with renewable energy there will also be more jobs available for tasks such as installation and maintenance for these sources.
